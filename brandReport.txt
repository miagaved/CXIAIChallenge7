As a Senior Design Critic, I must say that comparing the functional, information-dense design of Wikipedia to the sleek, innovative Samsung Style template reveals significant divergence. Wikipedia's design prioritizes accessibility, readability, and content over a branded aesthetic, while the Samsung template suggests a modern, professional, and visually cohesive experience.

Let's break down the comparison:

### Visual Gaps Analysis

1.  **Colors:**
    *   **Target:** The Samsung template strongly favors a clean palette of `#0c4da2` (Samsung Blue), `#000000` (Pure Black), and `#ffffff` (Pure White).
    *   **Website:** Wikipedia uses a broader range of muted grays (`#202122`, `#404244`, etc.) as primary text/background colors, with various shades of blue (`#36c`, `#3056a9`, `#233566`) for links and accents. While white (`#fff`) is present, the specific, vibrant Samsung blue is absent, and pure black is not used as prominently for text as a primary color, opting instead for dark grays. The overall color harmony is very different.

2.  **Fonts:**
    *   **Target:** `SamsungSharpSans` (a modern, geometric sans-serif) and `Arial` as a fallback.
    *   **Website:** Wikipedia predominantly uses generic `sans-serif` fonts, system fonts (`var(--font-family-system-sans)`), and some serif fonts (`Linux Libertine,Hoefler Text,Georgia,Times New Roman,Times,serif`). There is no `SamsungSharpSans` or even `Arial` explicitly listed as a primary font. This is a complete mismatch, indicating a vastly different typographic voice.

3.  **Button Style:**
    *   **Target:** `Rounded corners, blue background, white text`. This suggests prominent, inviting call-to-action elements.
    *   **Website:** Wikipedia's language selection buttons are typically more functional â€“ often with a white background, blue text (as hyperlinks), and generally sharp or very slightly rounded corners, with subtle borders. This is a direct stylistic opposition to the Samsung template.

4.  **Spacing & Tone:**
    *   **Target Tone:** "Innovative and professional." This typically implies generous whitespace, clean layouts, clear hierarchy, and a modern, often minimalistic aesthetic.
    *   **Website:** Wikipedia's design is highly functional, prioritizing information density and universal access. It generally has a more utilitarian spacing, which can appear somewhat constrained when compared to a sleek, modern, professional template. It feels more academic and encyclopedic rather than "innovative" in a design sense.

### 5 Specific Changes to Align with Samsung Style

1.  **Adopt Samsung's Primary Blue for Interactive Elements:** Update all prominent interactive elements (buttons, primary links) to use the vibrant `#0c4da2` as their background or accent color.
2.  **Implement Samsung-Aligned Typography:** Replace current fonts with `SamsungSharpSans` (or a similar geometric sans-serif like `Montserrat` or `Poppins` if `SamsungSharpSans` is proprietary/unavailable) as the primary font for headings and body text, falling back to `Arial`.
3.  **Redesign Buttons to Samsung Style:** Transform the main call-to-action buttons (e.g., language selection, search) to feature a solid blue background (`#0c4da2`), crisp white text (`#ffffff`), and distinctly rounded corners.
4.  **Standardize Text Color to Pure Black:** Change primary body text and heading colors from current dark grays to pure black (`#000000`) for a sharper, more professional contrast.
5.  **Increase Whitespace and Padding:** Introduce more generous `padding` and `margin` values around content blocks, between paragraphs, and within interactive elements to create a cleaner, less cluttered, and more breathable layout, aligning with an "innovative and professional" tone.

3.  **Redesign Buttons to Samsung Style:**

4.  **Standardize Text Color to Pure Black:**

    }
